Point of No Return in Ghana

The "Door of No Return" in Ghana refers to a specific passage in the walls of Elmina Castle (and also Cape Coast Castle) where enslaved Africans were forced through before being loaded onto ships for the transatlantic slave trade. It serves as a powerful and somber symbol of this dark period in history, representing the final point of departure from the African continent for many people.
